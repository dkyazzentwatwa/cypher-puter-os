Genre: quest desk management sim.

## Overview

Guildmaster Pocket puts you behind a tiny adventurer guild counter. You choose quests, assemble parties, manage class lanes, resolve staged missions, upgrade the hall, and deal with rivals.

## Goal Of A Run

Earn fame and charter progress without bankrupting the hall or destroying your roster. Strong guilds manage stress, injury, food, trust, rank, and rival pressure.

## Controls

Use number keys for visible choices. Move with `W/S`, `K/J`, or `;/.`. Page with `A/D`. Select with `Enter` or BtnA. Back out with `Q`, Backspace, Delete, or Tab.

## Screen Guide

### GUILD

GUILD is the weekly management view. Use it to check hall, clients, region, supply, and charter status.

### QUESTS

QUESTS sends adventurers into contracts such as rat cellar, moss road, coin vault, moon mine, ash keep, frost gate, signal ruin, and baron road.

### PARTY

PARTY picks class coverage. Fighter, scout, medic, mage, guard, bard, engineer, and mystic lanes all change outcome odds.

### ROSTER

ROSTER trains class lanes and improves long-term readiness.

### RESULT

RESULT resolves stage types such as combat, trap, social, travel, weird, boss, escort, and rescue.

### HALL

HALL upgrades infirmary, barracks, library, blacksmith, map room, and trophy wall.

### RIVALS

RIVALS handles pressure from other guilds. Rival choices can steal jobs, boost risk, or open challenge events.

## Core Loop

1. Check the guild week.
2. Pick a quest.
3. Choose a party with useful class coverage.
4. Resolve staged results.
5. Train roster lanes and upgrade the hall.
6. Watch rival pressure.

## Risk, Progress, And Endings

Fame and rank advance the guild. Stress, injury, and low trust create failure paths. Endings include great guild, hero graveyard, rival merger, quiet hall, treaty desk, and bankrupt hall.

## Tips For First Run

- Avoid sending under-covered parties to boss-heavy quests.
- Build the infirmary if injuries pile up.
- Food matters before long routes.
- Trust is harder to restore than gold.
- Use roster training before chasing high-rank contracts.
